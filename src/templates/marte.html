Planet Earth is the third planet orbiting the Sun (star of type G2V, who is in his youth according to stellar evolution), is a metallic terrestrial planet, classified in this category along with Mercury, Venus and Mars due to the composition of their nuclei.
The planet Earth is characterized by being the densest in the entire Solar System, by having the most intense magnetic field and the highest surface gravity of the rocky planets, since it has the highest rotation speed.

Planet Earth does not have rings. It has a natural satellite called "Selene" or more popularly known as Luna, it orbits only 384,400 km away from the surface, it is the fourth largest natural satellite in the Solar System. This contributes to the stabilization of the tilt of the Earth, as well as tidal changes.

  • Rotation. A rotating movement on its own axis, which intermittently exposes its surface to the sun and is the cause of days and nights.9

Planet Earth is made up of concentric layers of matter that extend toward the core from the surface. These layers are:

  • lithosphere. It extends from the surface (0 kilometers deep) to about 60 kilometers inwards, being the least dense layer of all and the only one that we can visit with concrete physical means. That's where the tectonic plates are, for example. The lithosphere is further divided into two distinct layers.
    • Cortex. It goes from 0 to 35 kilometers deep, being the layer where life is located, consisting mainly of solid silicates.
    • Upper mantle. It ranges from 35 to 60 kilometers deep, and is composed mostly of peridotite rocks, extremely basic, from which the basalts could come.
  • Earth's mantle. The mantle of the Earth goes from 35 kilometers deep, to 2890, that is, to the outer part of the core. It is the widest layer of the internal structure of the Earth, rich in silicates, magnesium and iron, all in a semi-solid state and of variable viscosity. Within the mantle is the inner mantle and also the asthenosphere.
    • Asthenosphere. A low-viscosity layer that comprises the upper part of the Earth's mantle, made up of silicate materials in a solid state and semi-molten or partially molten, depending on its proximity to the boiling magma. The tectonic plates move above the asthenosphere. This layer goes from 100 to 700 kilometers deep.
  • Nucleus. The terrestrial core is the "heart" of the planet, and is composed mostly of ferromagnetic metals (iron and nickel), divided into two stages:
    • External nucleus. Stretching from 2,890 kilometers to 5,100 kilometers deep, this liquid layer of highly viscous metal rests on the inner core and comprises mostly iron, with traces of lighter elements.
    • Inner core. The true center of the Earth is a core of solid metal, which rotates with a slightly higher angular speed than the rest of the planet, and which is responsible for the generation of its magnetosphere. It has a radius of approximately 1,255 kilometers and its composition is believed to be 70% iron and 30% nickel, along with minute portions of other heavy metals such as iridium, lead, and titanium.
