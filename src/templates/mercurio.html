MERCURY

Mercury is the planet in the solar system closest to the Sun and the smallest. It is part of the so-called inner planets and lacks natural satellites like Venus. Mercury is one of the four rocky or solid planets; that is, it has a rocky body, like the Earth. This planet is the smallest of the four, with a diameter of 4,879 km at the equator.
Mercury is made up of approximately 70% metallic elements and 30% silicates. The density of this planet is the second largest in the entire solar system, its value being 5430 kg/m³, only slightly less than the density of the Earth. Mercury's density can be used to deduce the details of its internal structure.

The surface of Mercury, like that of the Moon, has numerous meteorite impacts ranging from a few meters to thousands of kilometers. Some of the craters are relatively recent, a few million years old, and are characterized by the presence of a central peak.

Main Movements

  • - Rotation. A rotating movement on its own axis, which intermittently exposes its surface to the sun and is the cause of days and nights.
  • - Translation. This is the displacement of the planet along its solar orbit, describing more or less an ellipse as a path. Every time we celebrate a year, it marks one more revolution of the planet around the Sun
  • This small planet rotates slowly compared to Earth, so a day lasts a long time. Mercury takes 59 Earth days to make one complete rotation. A year on Mercury goes by fast. Because it is the closest planet to the Sun, it does not take long for it to cover the entire circumference. It completes one revolution around the Sun in just 88 Earth days. If you lived on Mercury, you would have a birthday party every three months!

GENERAL FEATURES OF MERCURY

  • - Mercury's atmosphere

    • Mercury's atmosphere is extremely thin, which is why it is so easy for meteors and asteroids to reach the surface and hit it. Its "atmosphere" is actually an exosphere, composed mostly of oxygen, sodium, helium, hydrogen and potassium and created by the effect of atoms detached from the surface and the impact of micrometeorites. Since surface temperatures are often extreme, atoms quickly and easily escape into space.
  • - Earth's mantle. The mantle of the Earth goes from 35 kilometers deep, to 2890, that is, to the outer part of the core. It is the widest layer of the internal structure of the Earth, rich in silicates, magnesium and iron, all in a semi-solid state and of variable viscosity. Within the mantle is the inner mantle and also the asthenosphere.

    • Asthenosphere. A low-viscosity layer that comprises the upper part of the Earth's mantle, made up of silicate materials in a solid state and semi-molten or partially molten, depending on its proximity to the boiling magma. The tectonic plates move above the asthenosphere. This layer goes from 100 to 700 kilometers deep.
  • - Nucleus. The terrestrial core is the "heart" of the planet, and is composed mostly of ferromagnetic metals (iron and nickel), divided into two stages:

    • External nucleus. Stretching from 2,890 kilometers to 5,100 kilometers deep, this liquid layer of highly viscous metal rests on the inner core and comprises mostly iron, with traces of lighter elements.
    • Inner core. The true center of the Earth is a core of solid metal, which rotates with a slightly higher angular speed than the rest of the planet, and which is responsible for the generation of its magnetosphere. It has a radius of approximately 1,255 kilometers and its composition is believed to be 70% iron and 30% nickel, along with minute portions of other heavy metals such as iridium, lead, and titanium.
