VENUS

Planet Earth is the third planet orbiting the Sun (star of type G2V, who is in his youth according to stellar evolution), is a metallic terrestrial planet, classified in this category along with Mercury, Venus and Mars due to the composition of their nuclei.
The planet Earth is characterized by being the densest in the entire Solar System, by having the most intense magnetic field and the highest surface gravity of the rocky planets, since it has the highest rotation speed.

Planet Earth does not have rings. It has a natural satellite called "Selene" or more popularly known as Luna, it orbits only 384,400 km away from the surface, it is the fourth largest natural satellite in the Solar System. This contributes to the stabilization of the tilt of the Earth, as well as tidal changes.

GENERAL FEATURES OF MERCURY

  • - Mercury's atmosphere

    • Mercury's atmosphere is extremely thin, which is why it is so easy for meteors and asteroids to reach the surface and hit it. Its "atmosphere" is actually an exosphere, composed mostly of oxygen, sodium, helium, hydrogen and potassium and created by the effect of atoms detached from the surface and the impact of micrometeorites. Since surface temperatures are often extreme, atoms quickly and easily escape into space.
  • - Mercury's surface

    • The surface of Mercury, like that of the Moon, has numerous meteorite impacts ranging from a few meters to thousands of kilometers. Some of the craters are relatively recent, a few million years old, and are characterized by the presence of a central peak. It seems that the oldest craters have had a very strong erosion, possibly due to large changes in temperature, which on a normal day range between 623 K (350 °C) during the day and 103 K (-170 °C) during the night. evening.
    • Like the Moon, Mercury appears to have undergone a period of intense bombardment by large meteorites about 4,000,000,000 (four billion) years ago. During this period of crater formation, Mercury received impacts on its entire surface, facilitated by the virtual absence of an atmosphere that could disintegrate or stop many of these rocks. During this time, Mercury was volcanically active, forming basins or depressions with lava in the interior of the planet and producing smooth plains similar to the seas or maria of the Moon; proof of this is the discovery by the MESSENGER probe of possible volcanoes.
  • - Magnetosphere

    • The study of Mercury's interaction with the solar wind has revealed the existence of a magnetosphere around the planet. The origin of this magnetic field is not known. In 2007, very precise ground-based radar observations demonstrated a wobble of the rotational axis compatible only with a partially molten core of the planet. A partially molten core with ferromagnetic materials could be the cause of its magnetic field.
